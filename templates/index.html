About Automotive Cybersecurity

Modern vehicles contain dozens of Electronic Control Units (ECUs) that control everything from engine management to infotainment systems. As vehicles become more connected, they also become more vulnerable to cyber attacks.

Threat Analysis and Risk Assessment (TARA) is a critical process in automotive cybersecurity that helps identify, evaluate, and mitigate potential security risks. This process is mandated by industry standards such as ISO 21434 and SAE J3061.

Key ECU Types

  • Engine Control Module (ECM)
  • Transmission Control Module (TCM)
  • Body Control Module (BCM)
  • Brake Control Module (BCM)
  • Telematics Control Unit (TCU)
  • Infotainment System

Common Attack Vectors

  • CAN bus vulnerabilities
  • Wireless interfaces (Bluetooth, Wi-Fi)
  • Cellular connections
  • OBD-II port access
  • Infotainment system exploits
  • Supply chain attacks
